# Giza around 1890: the pyramids, the Sphinx, and who was there

Research basis for making the **Giza plateau** an enterable first-person
"monument site" (TASKS point 273): what the three great pyramids and the Great
Sphinx actually **looked like** in ~1890, how they must read **distinct from the
game's Meroë (Nubian) pyramids** (design.md §4.4), what to refine in the existing
bird's-eye landmark and Cairo skyline builders (point 82,
`src/render/landmarks.ts` `buildGizaPyramids` / `buildSphinx`), and whether
**period-appropriate ambient people** belong at the site. Written before any code
so the monument is built from a cited picture rather than the modern,
fully-cleared postcard. Sibling of `docs/peoples-1890.md`,
`docs/climate-1890.md` and `docs/fauna-behaviour-1890.md`; it follows their
citation style and evidence discipline.

The load-bearing period fact is the **Sphinx's sand line**: in 1890 the Sphinx
was **not** the fully-excavated monument of the modern photograph — its body lay
buried, only the head and upper chest clear of the sand (§1.3). Getting that one
detail right is what makes the site read as 1890 rather than 2020.

Evidence markers, following the sibling docs' discipline:

| Marker | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **PERIOD** | pre-1910 eyewitness / record / photograph (traveller, survey, archive) |
| **FIELD** | measured archaeological / survey figure (Egyptology, Petrie and after) |
| **REVIEW** | secondary synthesis (encyclopaedic / reference accounts) |
| **INFERRED** | reasoned from the above; **not** directly attested — treat as a hypothesis |
| **MYTH** | folklore, explicitly **not** supported by evidence — recorded to be marked as such |
| **GAP** | not well documented at this scale — do not over-assert |

The accuracy bar is the sibling docs': a hedged answer beats a confident wrong
one, myth is labelled myth, and inference is labelled inference. Metric figures
below are the standard Egyptological ones; small variations between sources
(a metre here, a degree there) are within normal survey/erosion tolerance and do
not affect the game's silhouette.

## 1. How Giza looks

### 1.1 The three great pyramids — relative size and the stripped-core look

The plateau's three main pyramids stand in a **south-west diagonal row**
(Khufu north-east, Khafre centre, Menkaure south-west), each the tomb of an
Old-Kingdom pharaoh of the Fourth Dynasty.

- **Khufu / Cheops (the Great Pyramid)** — the largest by original height and by
  volume. Built to **~146.5 m** with a base of **~230 m** per side and a face
  slope of **~51.8° (51°50′)**; today ~138.5 m as the top courses and apex are
  gone
  ([Great Pyramid of Giza, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Giza);
  [Great Pyramid facts, Kiddle](https://kids.kiddle.co/Great_Pyramid_of_Giza))
  — FIELD.
- **Khafre / Chephren** — built slightly lower at **~143.5 m** on a base of
  **~215.5 m** per side, at a marginally steeper **~53° (53°10′)**
  ([Pyramid of Khafre, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Khafre))
  — FIELD. It **looks the taller of the two** from most viewpoints because it
  stands on **higher bedrock** (REVIEW/FIELD) — a real, renderable trick of the
  plateau, not an illusion to design around.
- **Menkaure / Mykerinos** — **much smaller**, built to only **~65–66 m** on a
  base of **~102–105 m** per side
  ([The three pyramids of Giza, VisitGiza](https://visitgiza.com/giza/travel-guides/historical-sites/the-three-pyramids-of-giza-khufu-khafre-and-menkaure))
  — FIELD. Its **lower courses were cased in red Aswan granite** (much survives),
  a distinct darker/pinker band at its base above the pale limestone core
  ([Pyramid of Menkaure, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyramid_of_Menkaure))
  — FIELD.

**The stripped-core look.** All three were originally sheathed in polished white
**Tura limestone** that made the faces smooth and gleaming. That casing was
**stripped over the centuries** — a 1303 earthquake loosened it and it was
carted off to build medieval Cairo (mosques, walls; the sultan an-Nasir's works
c. 1356 are the named case) — leaving the **stepped, rough core masonry** that
visitors have seen ever since, never meant to be exposed
([Apollo Magazine, Giza casing stones](https://apollo-magazine.com/great-pyramid-giza-egypt-casing-stone/);
[ScienceInsights, what happened to the limestone](https://scienceinsights.org/what-happened-to-the-limestone-on-the-pyramids/))
— REVIEW/FIELD. So in 1890 (and today) Khufu and Menkaure read as **tawny,
stepped, blunt-topped** masses — Khufu's true apex and top courses are **gone**,
leaving a small flat platform, not a sharp point — INFERRED-from-FIELD (the
missing-cap state is well attested; "blunt/flat top" is the readable consequence).

### 1.2 Khafre's casing cap — the one distinguishing visual

The single most useful visual cue on the plateau: **Khafre alone still carries a
cap of its original smooth Tura-limestone casing near the apex.** It is the
**only surviving casing on any Giza pyramid**, a pale, smooth-sided cone perched
on the stepped core — so distinctive that it looks "as if a second, smoother peak
were jammed on top of the first"
([Khafre casing at peak, Tripadvisor review](https://www.tripadvisor.com/ShowUserReviews-g294202-d308882-r752938492-Khafre_s_Pyramid-Giza_Giza_Governorate.html);
[Pyramid of Khafre history, Pyramid-of-Giza.com](https://www.pyramid-of-giza.com/pyramid-of-khafre/))
— PERIOD/REVIEW (the cap is visible in 19th-century photographs and survives
today). **This is the detail that lets a player tell Khafre from Khufu at a
glance**, and the game does not yet render it (§3).

### 1.3 The Great Sphinx — proportions and, crucially, the ~1890 sand line

The Sphinx is a single limestone statue carved from the plateau bedrock: a
**recumbent (couchant) lion's body with a human (pharaonic) head**, the head
wearing the **nemes** — the striped royal headcloth that flares out behind the
ears and down over the shoulders. It measures **~73 m long** (paw to tail),
**~20 m high** (base to crown), and **~19 m wide** across the rear haunches
([Great Sphinx of Giza, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Sphinx_of_Giza))
— FIELD. It faces **due east**. The **nose is already gone** in 1890 (lost
centuries earlier), so a period-correct face is **noseless** — PERIOD/REVIEW.

**The period state — buried to the shoulders.** This is the point the game must
get right. For most of recorded history the Sphinx lay **buried up to the neck /
shoulders in wind-blown sand**, only the head and the top of the chest showing;
Napoleon's 1798 expedition saw it that way. A century of partial digs
**exposed then re-buried** it:

- **1817** — Giovanni Caviglia's dig (the first modern one) cleared the **chest**
  — soon re-covered by sand ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Sphinx_of_Giza)) — PERIOD.
- **1853** — Auguste Mariette cleared around it (the sand returned) — PERIOD.
- **1885–1886** — Gaston Maspero, as head of the Antiquities Service, tried again,
  re-exposed the earlier work, then **abandoned the clearance** to the sand for
  want of resources
  ([Sacred Sites, Great Sphinx facts](https://sacredsites.com/africa/egypt/great_sphinx_facts.html);
  [Rare Historical Photos, Sphinx 1850–1940](https://rarehistoricalphotos.com/great-sphinx-giza-old-photographs/))
  — PERIOD/REVIEW.
- **1925–1936** — only Émile Baraize's long campaign finally cleared the **full
  body, paws and enclosure** and left the Sphinx we photograph today
  ([Émile Baraize, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89mile_Baraize))
  — FIELD/REVIEW.

**Net for 1890:** the Sphinx stood **buried to roughly the shoulders/upper
chest** — head, nemes and a little of the chest clear, the **paws, forelegs, lower
body, tail and enclosure all under sand**. Contemporary photographs (1850s–1900s)
show exactly this partly-emerged state
([Rare Historical Photos](https://rarehistoricalphotos.com/great-sphinx-giza-old-photographs/))
— PERIOD. Rendering the fully-cleared modern monument would be an anachronism of
~35 years.

### 1.4 Subsidiary structures — queens' pyramids, causeways, temples

Each king's pyramid is the centre of a **complex**, and several parts read at a
site scale:

- **Queens' / subsidiary pyramids** — small satellite pyramids: **three** in a
  row on the **east** side of Khufu (for queens/family; the "GI-a/b/c" group) and
  **three** on the **south** side of Menkaure
  ([Giza pyramid complex, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giza_pyramid_complex))
  — FIELD. Small, ruined, far lower than the main pyramids.
- **Causeways and temples** — each pyramid had a **mortuary temple** at its east
  foot linked by a stone **causeway** down to a **valley temple** at the desert
  edge. **Khafre's causeway and valley temple** run down toward the Sphinx, and
  the Sphinx sits beside Khafre's valley temple with its own small **Sphinx
  Temple** in front (largely sand-covered in 1890)
  ([Giza pyramid complex, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giza_pyramid_complex))
  — FIELD. At the game's scale these are **low ruined lines and blocks**, not
  standing architecture — dressing, not landmarks.

## 2. Giza vs the game's Meroë (Nubian) pyramids — enforce the contrast

The game already renders **Meroë** as a §4.4 cultural landmark
(`buildMeroePyramids`), correctly *steeper* than Giza. The point of this section
is to spell out the contrast so the two monuments can **never be mistaken for
each other** in-game. They are different building traditions ~2,000 km and many
centuries apart.

| Feature | **Giza** (Old Kingdom, ~2500 BC) | **Meroë / Nubian** (Kushite, ~300 BC–350 AD) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Height | **huge** — 65–147 m | **small** — ~6–30 m ([Nubian pyramids, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nubian_pyramids)) |
| Slope | **shallow, ~51–53°** | **steep, ~70°** ([Nubian pyramids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nubian_pyramids)) |
| Footprint | **broad** base (a similar-height Egyptian pyramid has a footprint ≥5× the Nubian one) | **narrow** — tall and slender off a small base |
| Grouping | **three** monumental masses, widely spaced on the diagonal | **dense clusters** of many small pyramids, some within touching distance |
| Attached chapel | mortuary temple + long causeway (separate, at the foot) | a small **offering chapel abutting the east base** of each pyramid |
| Material / tone | pale **limestone** core (Menkaure's granite skirt) | **sandstone** (with granite), warmer/redder |

All Nubian figures — FIELD, per [Nubian pyramids, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nubian_pyramids).

**The two cues that carry the distinction at any zoom:** (1) **slope** — Giza is
visibly *squat* (~52°), Meroë visibly *sharp* (~70°); and (2) **scale + count** —
Giza is a few giants, Meroë a crowd of small steep cones. The **offering chapel
on Meroë's east side** is the close-range tell. The current code already encodes
the slope contrast well (`buildGizaPyramids` uses height ≈ 0.64·base ⇒ ~52°;
`buildMeroePyramids` uses height ≈ 1.3·base ⇒ ~69°), so the main gap is the
**chapels** and keeping Meroë's individual pyramids clearly *smaller* than Giza's.

## 3. Recommendations for the bird's-eye view and Cairo's skyline (point 82)

The existing builders are a good base but read as **three identical tawny cones +
a fully-exposed Sphinx** — three refinements make them period- and
place-correct. All are cosmetic; none change the landmark's footprint or its
tests' anchors.

1. **Khafre's limestone cap (highest priority).** Give the **middle** pyramid
   (Khafre) a **smooth, paler cap** near its apex — a short smooth-sided cone of
   lighter limestone tone sitting on the stepped core — so Khafre is instantly
   distinguishable from Khufu and Menkaure (§1.2). This is the single most
   recognisable real feature of the plateau and is currently absent.
2. **Period-correct Sphinx sand line.** `buildSphinx` renders the whole couchant
   lion standing clear of the ground — the **modern** state. For 1890, **bury the
   body to the shoulders**: sink the torso/paws or wrap a **sand apron** so only
   the **head, nemes and upper chest** emerge (§1.3). Apply this **both** at the
   bird's-eye Giza landmark **and** in Cairo's western skyline. A **noseless**
   face is the correct period detail (optional at travel scale). Keep the Sphinx
   east of Khafre, facing east.
3. **Stepped, blunt-topped cores for Khufu & Menkaure.** Optional but cheap:
   read the two casing-stripped pyramids as **slightly stepped and flat/blunt at
   the apex** (Khufu's top is missing) rather than as sharp smooth points, and
   give **Menkaure a darker granite skirt** at its base. Reinforces the
   casing-vs-core story and the Giza-vs-Meroë tonal contrast.

**Bird's-eye view specifically:** the same three refinements apply — at travel
zoom the **Khafre cap** and the **squat ~52° profile** are what make the cluster
read as *Giza* (not Meroë), and the **half-buried Sphinx** is what makes it read
as *1890*. Nice-to-have dressing (low priority): a couple of tiny **queens'
pyramids** beside Khufu/Menkaure and faint **causeway lines** — only if they
don't clutter the silhouette. Sharpen Meroë in the same pass by adding the small
**east-side offering chapels** (§2).

## 4. People at Giza in ~1890 — yes, add period ambient walkers

The evidence is **strong**: by 1890 Giza sat at the centre of a booming organised
tourism economy, and the plateau was **peopled**, not empty.

- **The Thomas Cook era.** Thomas Cook & Son ran mass tours up the Nile and to
  the pyramids from the 1880s; in **1890** the firm sold over 3¼ million tickets
  and its Egypt operation (Nile steamers, guided excursions) was in full swing
  ([Smithsonian, Thomas Cook and global tourism](https://www.smithsonianmag.com/history/how-alcohol-hating-english-preacher-founded-global-tourism-180963016/);
  [Cook's Fall Tours to Egypt, Season 1890, Thomas Cook Archive](https://www.masstourism.amdigital.co.uk/Documents/Details/Cook-s-Fall-Tours-to-Egypt-and-Palestine--Season-1890/TCA_B_Bx1888_Egypt_1890))
  — PERIOD. The **Mena House Hotel** at the foot of the plateau opened as a hotel
  **c. 1886–1887**, putting Western tourists on the doorstep — REVIEW.
- **The local guide economy.** The village of **Nazlet el-Samman**, at the foot
  of the pyramids, made its living serving visitors: **cameleers and horse/donkey
  men** for rides around the monuments, and **guides who assisted (and pressed)
  tourists up the Great Pyramid** for *backsheesh* — a famous local could scale
  the Great Pyramid in minutes
  ([Nazlet el-Samman: a community dedicated to tourism](https://rehabsaadblog.wordpress.com/2012/12/31/nazlet-el-samman-a-community-dedicated-to-tourism/))
  — REVIEW (the community's tourism vocation is period-rooted; the named
  characters are 20th-century colour). Contemporary photographs routinely show
  **tourists on camels and donkeys, robed local guides, and dragomen** posed at
  the Sphinx and pyramids
  ([Rare Historical Photos, Sphinx 1850–1940](https://rarehistoricalphotos.com/great-sphinx-giza-old-photographs/))
  — PERIOD.
- **The cast, for ambient walkers:** robed local **guides / dragomen** and
  **Bedouin cameleers**; **donkey-boys** with their mounts; a scatter of
  **Western tourists** in 1890s dress (pith helmets, long skirts); **camels and
  donkeys** standing or ambling. A handful is enough to read "the pyramids are a
  visited place in 1890." Reuse the settlement walker/figure system rather than
  new mesh work.
- **GAP / restraint:** exact daily numbers and precise dress detail at *this*
  scale are not worth over-specifying — keep the crowd **sparse and suggestive**,
  a few figures and animals, not a throng.

## 5. Myths to keep out

Recorded so they are **not** built as fact:

- **"Pharaoh's curse."** The curse-of-the-mummy / pyramid-curse trope is a
  **20th-century media invention** (crystallised around the 1922 Tutankhamun
  discovery), not an 1890 belief and not a fact — **MYTH**. No curse mechanic.
- **Mystic alignment / "lost knowledge" folklore.** Orion-alignment and
  ancient-super-science claims are modern pseudo-archaeology — **MYTH**. The
  cardinal (north-aligned) orientation of the pyramids and the Sphinx's due-east
  facing are the only real, citable orientation facts (FIELD).
- **The Sphinx's nose "shot off by Napoleon's cannon."** **MYTH** — the nose was
  already missing centuries before Napoleon; sketches predating him show it gone.
  In-game the 1890 Sphinx is simply **noseless**, with no story attached.

## 6. Recommendation and implementation brief

**Recommendation:** build the Giza monument site from the **period** picture, not
the modern postcard. The two facts that carry the whole scene are **Khafre's
pale limestone cap** (the pyramid tell) and the **Sphinx buried to the
shoulders** (the 1890 tell); the Giza-vs-Meroë contrast rides on **slope and
scale**; and the plateau should be **lightly peopled** with Thomas-Cook-era
tourists and local Bedouin guides. Refine the existing point-82 builders in
place — no new landmark, no test-anchor changes.

**Implementation brief (docs-only spec; no code here):**

- **Khufu / Cheops:** the biggest mass; **tawny stepped core**, **blunt/flat top**
  (missing apex), ~52° squat profile. Base ≈ 230, height ≈ 146 (game-scale ratio
  height ≈ 0.64·base — already correct).
- **Khafre / Chephren:** second, on **higher ground so it reads as tall**; its
  signature **smooth pale limestone CAP near the apex** on the stepped core — the
  one must-have new cue. Same ~52–53° profile.
- **Menkaure / Mykerinos:** clearly the **smallest** (~0.45× Khufu); optional
  **darker granite skirt** at the base.
- **Great Sphinx:** couchant lion + **nemes**-headed human face, facing **east**,
  **noseless**; **buried to the shoulders** — only head, nemes and upper chest
  above a sand line, paws/body/enclosure under sand (the period sand line; do
  **not** render the fully-cleared modern body). Same half-buried treatment in
  Cairo's western skyline.
- **Giza-vs-Meroë contrast to enforce:** Giza = a **few huge squat (~52°)**
  masses with Khafre's cap; Meroë = a **dense cluster of small steep (~70°)**
  sandstone cones with **east-side offering chapels**. Keep Meroë's pyramids
  visibly smaller than any Giza pyramid; add the chapels to sharpen it.
- **Skyline / bird's-eye:** carry the Khafre cap and the half-buried Sphinx into
  both views; optional low-priority dressing — tiny **queens' pyramids** and faint
  **causeway lines**.
- **Ambient people (add, sparse):** a few **robed local guides / Bedouin
  cameleers**, **donkey-boys**, and **1890s Western tourists**, with **camels and
  donkeys** — reuse the existing figure/walker system; keep it a handful, not a
  crowd.
- **Keep out (MYTH):** no pharaoh's curse, no mystic alignment, no
  Napoleon's-cannon nose story.
